What is netball?

Netball is a ball sport played by two teams of seven players, played indoor and outdoor, using a circle ball and two hoops. Netball was first played in england in 1895 at madame ostenburg's college. Netball is an invention that traces its roots to basketball, it was adapted for female students with the aim of maintaing female entiquette.

Netball court

A netball court is just a bit bigger than a basketball court, up to 30.5 (or 100 ft) long and 15.25 metres (50 ft) wide.

Understanding the positions in Netball

When playing against the other team these are your buddies to stick with from the oppisite team.

GA-GD, GS-GK, C-C, WD-WA

Goal Shoot (GS): Works in and around the semi-circle with the GA to score goals

Goal Attack (GA): Works with GS to score goals, only allowed one third of the court.

Wing Attack (WA): Flanks the offensive players giving them shooting opportunities

Centre (C): Links the defence and the attack, allowed anywhere but inside the semi-circles.

Wing Defence (WD): Prevents WA from passing and to look for interceptions

Goal Defence (GD): Intercepts the ball and prevent passes to the GA

Goal Keeper (GK): Prevents the GA/GS from scoring goals within the semi-circle and works with GD

Understanding the rules/game of netbal

A netball game lasts for 60 minutes, the 60min is splited into 4 quartes, each quarter lasting 15 minutes. taking breaks between each quarter will take 3 minutes, but in between second quarter and the third (which is half time) will last five minutes. There are 7 players in a netball game, but can caryy on with minimum of 5 players. A netball game is played in a court with a ball and two hoops (goals). The aim of the game is to see who scores more points of each teams.
